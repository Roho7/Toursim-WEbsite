Cusines

All You Can Eat!


Luqaimat

These warm dumplings have a doughnut-like flavour and texture. Fresh batches are made everyday and served with a sticky date sauce and sesame seeds. Local Bites Café in Jumeirah is an excellent place to taste them in the middle of the morning with a cup of steaming coffee.

Knafeh

Originating in Palestine, this pastry delicacy has become a solid favourite among UAE residents. It's best eaten right away because it's created with sour cheese, crisp sugar syrup, and dough. Qwaider Al Nabulsi in Deira is one of the most popular venues to try it. During Ramadan, orders for this dessert might number in the thousands each day, as people ask for it for iftar - the meal eaten by Muslims at dusk to break their fast.

Camel

Emiratis have always avoided eating camel meat, but modern chefs in the city are increasingly experimenting with everything from camel sliders to camel biryani, burgers, and stews. Camel milk, which is somewhat saltier than cow's milk, contains more protein, less cholesterol, and more vitamin C and iron. Don't want to drink the entire glass? Instead, try camel milk ice cream. Arab cafés throughout the city provide a variety of flavours, including pistachio, chocolate, and date.

Turkish Cocktails

Dubai is hardly a dry city, and its cocktail scene is becoming more vibrant. Ruya's Anatolian Fizz, prepared with sparkling wine, pomegranate molasses, lemon, rose and raspberry, is a must-try. Ruya's drinks incorporate traditional Turkish flavour such as hibiscus, rose, pomegranate, honey, spices, citrus, and mint.

Samboosa

These hot pastry appetisers, like many other cuisines in the Emirates, were influenced by flavours and methods from across the Arabian Sea in India. Some are loaded with meat, veggies, and spices, but the most famous local variety is stuffed with three different kinds of cheese.

Arabic coffee and dates

Arabic coffee is provided free of charge everywhere in the UAE, from government buildings to hotel lobbies. Head to Café Bateel and enjoy the Bateel specialty qahwa, a traditional Arabic coffee brewed with lightly roasted beans and cardamom and served with organic local dates.
