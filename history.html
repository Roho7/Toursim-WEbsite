Since Time Immemorial


Burj Khalifa

BURJ KHALIFA: The Burj Khalifa, located in Dubai, is the world's tallest building. It formally opened on January 4, 2010, however the project had been in the works for some years prior. The Burj Khalifa was envisaged in the late 1990s, when Dubai's leaders were seeking for methods to diversify the city's economy and increase its global profile. The project was renamed Burj Khalifa in honour of Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, President of the United Arab Emirates and ruler of Abu Dhabi. The Burj Khalifa's construction began in 2004 and took six years to complete. The design of the structure was inspired by Islamic architecture, and it includes a sequence of setbacks and tapering shapes that help to lessen wind loads and make it more stable. The building's outside is clad in a high-tech glass and aluminium curtain wall, which reflects the surrounding scenery and provides breathtaking views of Dubai. The Burj Khalifa is no longer just a sign of Dubai's affluence and ambition; it has also become an iconic symbol of contemporary architecture and a tribute to Dubai's commitment to continually expanding the boundaries of what is possible.

Palm Jumeirah

PALM JUMEIRAH: Palm Jumeirah is a man-made island off the coast of Dubai that is designed like a palm tree. It is one of three artificial islands built by Nakheel, a Dubai-based property developer. Palm Jumeirah was first planned in the early 2000s as a means of increasing beachfront property and boosting Dubai's tourism economy. The island's construction began in 2001 and was completed in 2006. Palm Jumeirah was built by dredging millions of cubic metres of sand from the bottom of the Persian Gulf and shaping it into the shape of a palm tree. The island is home to extravagant hotels, private villas, and a variety of recreational and entertainment opportunities, like water parks and shopping malls.

The Grand Mosque

The Grand Mosque of Dubai is one of the United Arab Emirates' largest mosques. It was finished in 1998, and Sheikh Rashid bin Saeed Al Maktoum, the former ruler of Dubai, commissioned its construction. The mosque is constructed in the classical Islamic style, with white marble domes and minarets that rise up to 70 metres in height. It can hold up to one thousand worshippers at once and has elaborate floral and geometric motifs typical of Islamic art. The Grand Mosque is a renowned tourist attraction as well as a place of worship for Muslims in Dubai. The Grand Mosque of Dubai is a stunning and inspirational example of Islamic architecture, as well as a tribute to Dubai's dedication to religious acceptance and tolerance. It is a must-see place for everyone interested in discovering more about Dubai and the UAE's rich cultural legacy.
